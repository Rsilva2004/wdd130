Pará

Pará is the name of a Brazilian state and derives from the Tupi term "pa’ra", which means "river-sea" or "river as big as the sea". Its capital is Belém, and the state has 144 municipalities. Historically, the region was explored by the Spanish Francisco de Orellana and later integrated into the Captaincy of Maranhão, evolving into the Captaincy of Grão-Pará and Rio Negro.
